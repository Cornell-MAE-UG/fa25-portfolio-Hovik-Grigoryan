For my MAE3240 - Heat Transfer course in Cornell I have done a number of assignments, out of which Problem Set 3 was perhaps the most rewarding and educational. 
To solve the questions in Problem Set 3, I had to combine various heat transfer analysis techniques. For instance, I have defined three different control volumes and surfaces within question 2, while tracking which quantities remain constant. For question 1 I performed an analysis on a small section of a fluid (reducing the question). Thus, working on problems while combining various techniques of heat transfer analysis has been a rewarding experience, and it refined my skills in heat transfer analysis. 
Question 2 also included graphing with MATLAB, which allowed me to make a mental connection between the process of heat transfer through conduction and convection. Graphically representing the process helped me understand heat transfer on a deeper, more intuitive level. Question 3 also helped me get a practice with handling heat transfer questions through a different method of analysis, specifically thermal resistances.
