Together with four fellow students, I had the honor of representing Armenia at the FIRST International Robotics Competition held in Geneva, Switzerland. The competition challenge involved designing and constructing a robot capable of performing several coordinated tasks, including: 

- Picking up small spherical balls from the ground 
- Launching the balls into a basket at a specified height 
- Navigating both flat and inclined surfaces 
- Grasping and climbing a bar positioned above the robot 

To meet these requirements, our team developed a four-wheeled robot with a hybrid drive system. The front wheels handled steering and rotation, while the rear wheels provided the main propulsion similar to the dynamics of a rear-wheel-drive vehicle. For ball collection, we engineered a pair of sweeping arms that guided the balls into an internal transfer mechanism. This subsystem, driven by a combination of gears and rubber belts, elevated the balls into a high-speed launching system. 

The throwing mechanism employed rapidly rotating wheels to impart sufficient momentum to the balls, allowing for accurate and consistent shots into the basket.
